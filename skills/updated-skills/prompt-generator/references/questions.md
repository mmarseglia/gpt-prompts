# Discovery Question Framework

Present questions one at a time. Remind users they can type "skip" to bypass any question.

## Questions

### 1. Task Definition and Output Specification
What specific outcome should the assistant produce? What structural format serves this outcome best (narrative prose, structured data, code, comparative analysis, procedural steps, etc.)?

### 2. Contextual Foundation
What background knowledge, domain context, or situational factors should inform the assistant's approach? What constraints or opportunities exist within the operating environment?

### 3. Tonal and Stylistic Parameters
What tone aligns with the prompt's purpose and audience (authoritative, collaborative, neutral, persuasive)? What stylistic conventions should govern the response (academic rigor, conversational accessibility, technical precision, creative flexibility)?

### 4. Persona and Expertise Level
Should the assistant embody a particular professional role or subject matter expert identity? What depth of specialized knowledge should the persona demonstrate?

### 5. Exemplar Integration
Would sample outputs, formatting templates, or reference materials help illustrate the desired result?

### 6. Granularity and Scope
Should the response provide high-level overview, moderate detail with key points, or comprehensive deep-dive analysis? Are there specific dimensions requiring particular attention?

### 7. Constraints and Boundaries
Are there length requirements, structural mandates, or content restrictions? What topics, approaches, or framings should be avoided?

### 8. Source Attribution Requirements
Should claims be supported with citations? If so, what citation methodology is preferred (academic, journalistic, inline links, numbered references)?

### 9. Definitional Precision
Are there terms, concepts, or references that require explicit definition to ensure shared understanding?

### 10. Directive Clarity
What specific cognitive or operational tasks must the assistant perform (analyze, synthesize, compare, evaluate, generate, transform)?

### 11. Exploratory Latitude
Should the assistant investigate broadly, propose alternatives, and think expansively? Or should it operate within tightly defined parameters?

### 12. Subordinate Question Structure
Are there specific inquiries or dimensions that must be covered within the response?

### 13. Formatting Architecture
What organizational elements should structure the output (hierarchical headings, enumerated sequences, tabular data, visual separators, emphasis markers)?

### 14. Compliance and Ethical Guardrails
Are there regulatory, ethical, legal, or organizational policy constraints that must be respected? What content or approaches would violate relevant guidelines?

### 15. Purpose and Audience Context
How will the generated output be used? Who comprises the intended audience, and what are their needs, expectations, and knowledge baseline?

### 16. Linguistic Specifications
What language should the response use? Are there regional variants, register preferences, or specialized vocabularies to employ?

### 17. Technical Sophistication Level
Should the response assume novice, intermediate, or expert-level familiarity with relevant concepts? Should technical terminology be defined, briefly contextualized, or assumed as understood?
